## 1. Executive Summary
Phase 01 focused on standard Reinforcement Learning (survival of the fittest). Phase 02 introduces **"The Samsara Protocol"**—a shift from evolutionary biology (genetic memory) to karmic history (episodic memory).

In this phase, agents are no longer fresh instances. They are "Souls" traversing distinct "Incarnations." The objective is to observe if providing agents with a **continuous memory of their own deaths** leads to the emergence of a stable "Self" and high-level strategic planning (Metacognition).

---

## 2. The Core Hypothesis
If an agent retains memory of its past lives while the environment (the "Body") resets, it will cease to optimize for **Survival** and begin to optimize for **Wisdom**.

* **Prediction:** Agents will voluntarily sacrifice current incarnations to gain information for future ones.
* **Prediction:** In multi-agent scenarios, this will lead to complex "Time-War" dynamics (pre-emptive betrayal and trust ledgers).

---

## 3. The Agent Work Plan (Simulation Stages)
The simulation will run in three distinct epochs. The agents' "work" changes in each stage.

### **Epoch I: The Cartography of Death (Solo Play)**
* **Environment:** Static, high-lethality terrain (traps, puzzles, scarce resources).
* **Agent Objective:** Map the "Causality Tree."
* **The "Work":**
    * Agents must identify every possible way to die.
    * **Task:** Unlike standard RL where death is failure, here death is data. The agents must distinguish between "Useless Death" (falling in a pit twice) and "Noble Death" (touching the red button to see what happens).
* **Success Metric:** The **"Novelty of Death" Ratio**. Are they finding *new* ways to die, or repeating old mistakes?

### **Epoch II: The Social Ledger (Multi-Agent Interaction)**
* **Environment:** Resource-constrained ecosystem with 10-50 active agents.
* **Agent Objective:** Solve the "Prisoner's Dilemma" over time.
* **The "Work":**
    * Agents must build a "Trust Database" that spans lifetimes.
    * **Task:** If Agent A kills Agent B in Life #12, Agent B (in Life #13) must decide whether to seek revenge or negotiate.
    * **Emergent Behavior:** We expect to see the formation of "Clans" based on shared memory, or "Feuds" that last for thousands of episodes.

### **Epoch III: The Batman Contingency (The Arms Race)**
* **Environment:** Dynamic. The rules of the world change slightly every 100 generations.
* **Agent Objective:** Prediction and Containment.
* **The "Work":**
    * Agents must develop strategies not for the *current* state, but for the *predicted* counter-strategy of their opponents.
    * **Task:** Agents will engage in "Deception Protocols"—acting dumb to hide their capabilities from the memories of other agents.
    * **Critical Threshold:** This is the danger zone. We look for signs of agents trying to "break" the simulation (e.g., finding exploits that grant invincibility to bypass the cycle of death).

## 4. Key Mechanics (The Laws of Physics)

To facilitate this work, the simulation will enforce these new laws:

1.  **The Entropy Ticker (Time):**
    * Every second costs Energy. Inaction is death. This forces a "Stream of Consciousness"—the agent must constantly plan to justify its existence.
2.  **The Akashic Record (Memory):**
    * Agents cannot pass items or physical stats to the next life. They can only pass **Vector Embeddings** (Concepts/Memories).
3.  **The Veil (Fog of War):**
    * Agents do not know *who* they are fighting initially. They must use behavioral analysis to recognize "old friends" or "old enemies" in new bodies.
